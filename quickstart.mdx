This guide walks you through signing up, logging in, and saving your first note. The whole process takes less than five minutes.

## Before you begin

You only need a web browser and an email address. No installation required to get started — CloudBook runs at [list-app.vercel.app](https://list-app.vercel.app).

## Create your account and write your first note

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create an account">
    Go to [list-app.vercel.app](https://list-app.vercel.app) and click **Sign up**. Enter your email address and choose a password, then click **Create account**.

    <Note>
      After you register, CloudBook sends a welcome email to the address you provided. The email contains a cancellation link that lets you undo the registration within 24 hours — useful if someone signed up using your address without your knowledge.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Log in to your account">
    On the login page, enter your email and password, then click **Log in**.

    CloudBook authenticates your credentials and returns a JWT token, which is stored securely in your browser session. This token identifies you for all subsequent requests — you won't need to log in again until the session expires.

    <Tip>
      Keep your password strong and unique. A compromised account gives an attacker access to your encrypted notes and the ability to request decryption through the API.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the editor">
    After logging in, you land on your notes dashboard. Click **New note** to open the in-browser editor.

    The editor gives you a distraction-free writing area. You can:

    - Give your note a title using the title field at the top
    - Write and format your content in the main body area
    - Use the toolbar to apply bold, italic, headings, lists, and more
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write and save your first note">
    Type a title and some content, then click **Save**.

    CloudBook encrypts your note automatically using AES before storing it. You don't need to do anything special — encryption happens in the background every time you save.

    <Note>
      The CloudBook server encrypts your note content with AES before writing it to the database. Only the encrypted ciphertext is ever stored, so your plaintext content is never persisted.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Access your notes">
    Your saved notes appear in the notes dashboard. Click any note to open and read it — CloudBook decrypts it automatically when you load it.

    You can return to your notes from any browser by logging in with the same account. Your encrypted notes are stored in the cloud and sync across all your devices.

    <Tip>
      CloudBook is also available as a native app for Android and Windows. See the [download page](/download) to get the app for your device.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>
